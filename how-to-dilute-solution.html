How to Dilute a Solution

Diluting a solution means reducing its concentration by adding more solvent. The standard formula used in chemistry is C1V1 = C2V2, where:

  • C1 = initial concentration
  • V1 = initial volume
  • C2 = final concentration
  • V2 = final volume

Step by step guide:

  1. Decide the final concentration (C2) and volume (V2) you need.
  2. Use the formula C1V1 = C2V2 to calculate how much of the original solution (V1) is needed.
  3. Add the correct amount of solvent to reach the final volume (V2).

For example, to dilute 2M NaCl to 0.5M in 200 mL, leave V1 empty in our calculator and it will give you the amount of original solution needed.
